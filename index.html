The Story

Snozz was born on a late night tube home. After a long shift, exhausted and half asleep, I missed my stop. Again. I wanted something that actually worked for me. So I built Snozz.

The Founder

It was late. The kind of late that sits heavy on your shoulders after a long shift, when your eyes are already half-closed before you've even found a seat.

The tube rocked gently. London blurred past the windows. And somewhere on the way home, I fell asleep. I woke up at the end of the line. Again.

I searched the App Store. Surely someone had solved this.

So I built one. For everyone. I had never written a single line of code in my life.

The problems came one after another. Some small, some that consumed entire evenings — all of them solvable if I was willing to sit with the confusion long enough. Late nights. One laptop. No team. No funding. Just the belief that if this problem was real for me, it was real for millions of other people sitting in warm carriages across this city, drifting without meaning to.

Every obstacle had a solution. The solutions just required patience. So I kept going.

"The app does one thing. It does it well. It wakes you up when you're close to your stop — even with your screen locked, even with your phone in your pocket, even when you're deep in the kind of sleep that only a warm carriage and a long day can produce."

Snozz was built by someone who had every reason not to build it. And did it anyway.

— The Founder, London 2026
